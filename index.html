I study how technical systems encode meaning, misrepresent people, and distribute harm. My work sits at the intersection of AI governance, algorithmic fairness, contextual modeling, and Ubuntu-informed approaches to accountability and design.

Research Interests

Algorithmic fairness; contextual and relational modeling; AI governance and accountability; decolonial approaches to machine ethics; participatory and cross-cultural HRI design; voice-based perception and trust; legal and institutional design for high-stakes automation.

Current Work

  • Composite Vulnerability Persona (CVP) — a context-sensitive audit and governance framework evaluating how AI systems behave across intersecting vulnerabilities using relational, participatory and quantitative modeling.
  • Machine Ethics Curriculum — co-designed interdisciplinary ethics modules implemented across F&M College and published at the ACM Technical Symposium on Computer Science Education.
  • Virtual Reality & Memory — experimental study on immersive environments and memory recall, integrating Azure ML sentiment and behavioral pattern analysis.
  • Social Robotics & Perception — interactive Misty II robot experiments examining affect, trust, and interaction cues through Python-based event detection, pose control, and a custom Flask-Supabase sentiment dashboard.

Selected Projects

  • Hospital Navigation Agent (Python): Search-based autonomous agent implementing A*, BFS, admissible heuristics & state-space modeling.
  • AI in Admissions Study: Designed between-subjects experiment on susceptibility to AI-generated suitability scores; ran regression and inference tests.
  • Sentiment Modeling for VR Study: Applied Azure Python SDK, text analytics, emotional scoring & affective trajectory tracking.
  • CARES Lab Research: PCA, mixed-effects models, variance decomposition and multi-survey evaluation of ethical reasoning indicators across modules.
