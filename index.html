About

I study how technical systems encode meaning, misrepresent people, and distribute harm. My work brings together AI governance, algorithmic fairness, and an Afrocentric relational ethic grounded in Ubuntu.

I think about AI through three commitments: centering communities who have been treated as expendable data points, treating context and structure as part of the technical problem rather than an afterthought, and building spaces where people co-create the knowledge that steers the systems around them.

Right now that shows up through work on the Composite Vulnerability Persona (CVP), an Ubuntu-informed audit framework for AI systems; an interdisciplinary Machine Ethics course that treats students as co-authors of ethics curricula; and projects in virtual reality and human–robot interaction that examine how people experience memory, trust, and voice when machines enter the loop.

Research Interests

I am interested in the representational and inferential assumptions that govern how AI clusters, ranks, and assesses people, and in mitigating their impact on marginalized communities through relational and participatory design.

  • Algorithmic fairness in public and private institutions
  • Contextual and relational modeling for high-stakes automation
  • AI governance, regulation, and institutional accountability
  • Decolonial and Afrocentric approaches to machine ethics
  • Voice, affect, and trust in human–robot interaction and AI-mediated communication
  • Participatory design for AI and HRI; student-led ethics curricula

Selected Projects

Composite Vulnerability Persona (CVP)

An Ubuntu-informed design audit and governance framework that stress-tests AI systems across intersecting axes such as race, gender, class, immigration status, geography, disability, and digital access. The CVP combines participatory risk mapping with quantitative modeling to estimate relational harm for different personas.

Teaching & Learning Machine Ethics

Co-created an interdisciplinary Machine Ethics course with 21 students and seven faculty across non-CS departments. The course integrates Ubuntu, Afro-feminism, and Confucian ethics with technical topics in AI. As lead analyst, I built PCA pipelines and mixed-effects models in R to study how students’ ethical reasoning shifted across modules.

VR, Memory, and Sentiment

Designed a VR study on how immersive environments shape memory and emotional framing. Used Python and Azure’s sentiment tools to analyze narrative recall, linking affective tone to memory detail and perspective.

Misty Robot: Touch, Voice, and Trust

Built interactive behaviors for the Misty robot to study how touch, posture, and voice shape user affect during campus visits. Implemented touch events, poses, LED states, and logging in Python with the MistyPy SDK, and prototyped a sentiment dashboard with JavaScript and Flask.

Search-Based Hospital Agents

Implemented search-based autonomous agents in Python for a simulated hospital environment (DIS). Formalized navigation tasks, designed admissible heuristics, and evaluated agent performance across transport and delivery scenarios.
